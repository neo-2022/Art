## 1. Purpose
This document explains the outward visual language of Art. It is not about internal contracts or CI. It exists to keep the product visually coherent both in the operational UI and in customer-facing presentation.

## 2. Core visual idea
Art must not look like:
- a generic enterprise dashboard,
- a neon sci-fi attraction,
- a collage of unrelated component libraries.

Art should look like:
- a serious engineering system,
- a premium but disciplined product,
- an interface that communicates depth, control, and trust.

## 3. Base image
The Art visual language can be described as:
- a strict dark foundation,
- noble gold accents,
- depth and atmosphere,
- minimal visual noise,
- maximum readability under pressure.

This can be referred to as `Operational Dark Gold` or `Aero Ops`.

## 4. Two visual layers
### 4.1 Operational layer
The real working layer for engineers.

Requirements:
- high readability,
- instant state differentiation,
- no unnecessary effects by default,
- truth modes are never conveyed by color alone,
- long operational sessions remain usable.

### 4.2 Atmospheric layer
The layer of scale, identity, and atmosphere.

Requirements:
- reinforces Flow/3D exploration,
- creates a sense of system depth,
- never overrides the operational layer,
- automatically downgrades under perf pressure or reduced motion.

## 5. Flow/3D image
Flow/3D in Art is not “space for the sake of space”. It is an engineering projection of a live system.

Visual metaphors:
- flows as data air routes,
- clusters as clouds or masses of events,
- nodes as infrastructure anchors,
- highlights driven by truth mode, severity, confidence, and active context.

## 6. Why this matters to ordinary users
Even without knowing terms like DNA, audit, or lineage, users still notice:
- whether the interface is understandable,
- whether it feels trustworthy,
- whether it looks like a serious product or an unfinished prototype.

A strong visual language is not decoration. It reduces anxiety, speeds up onboarding, and makes the uniqueness of Art visible without a technical lecture.

## 7. Product showcase layer
Art needs not only a working UI, but also a showable product story:
- hero screen,
- three-tier product diagram,
- `event -> DNA -> evidence -> claim -> decision` chain,
- surfaces walkthrough,
- investigation scenario,
- Flow/3D demonstration as an engineering projection, not a visual gimmick.

## 8. What is forbidden
- using gold as the default body-text color,
- conveying truth mode by color alone,
- enabling cinematic 3D by default in operational response,
- merging warning semantics with brand-highlight semantics,
- mixing the production UI and the presentation layer so that they damage each other.

## 9. What still needs to be done
To complete this layer, the project still needs:
- a dedicated showcase spec,
- client-facing demo scenarios,
- a brand matrix for screens and artifacts,
- motion/demo/video/interactive prototype rules.
